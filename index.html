Tell us your Story

We all have a story. It has a beginning, a middle, and an end. It has meaning. Some of us may benefit from the presence of an active listener, who serves as our guide through the meaningful moments of our lives. This individual helps us create our narrative, and then compiles it into a crystalized mirror of ourselves. A memory that will last beyond our time on Earth. The result of this interview is a sturdily-bound book containing our words of legacy. Getting your words down on paper involves four steps.

Step 1

We provide sample questions to give you an idea of what you may want to talk about in the interview.

Step 2

The Interview. At a convenient time a team member will set things up to interview you at home.

Step 3

You will have a chance to read a rough draft and make edits if you so choose. Add more stories!

Step 4

You will receive a finalized copy in book or video form. Many choose to leave this to their family.

Leave a Legacy

How will I know what to say? Well, some have used this opportunity to explain how they did the best they could with what they had. Others as a form of “love letter” to their family. Still others take time to describe their philosophy on life, or lessons they have learned that might benefit future generations. It is wide open, and we can gravitate toward areas that interest you the most. Words convey to others who you are as a person, and many have found that this interview enhances their dignity in the eyes of their family.

Dignity Therapy

Dignity Therapy is closely associated with Hospice and Palliative Care. It was conceived by a team in Canada lead by Dr. Chochinov as a way to address the sense of lost dignity perceived by patients with serious illnesses. A Model of Dignity emerged once they began to interview patients. As one might expect, dignity is defined in a social setting. Simply put, it means that we hold the respect of other human beings, whether it be family, social groups, or the medical team members caring for us. Several clinical trials have demonstrated the benefits of Dignity Therapy.

Patients found it helpful. It increased their sense of dignity, and bolstered their dignity in the eyes of their families.

Chochinov.Lancet Onc 2011

Significantly higher levels of hope - renewed sense of purpose, decreased feelings of isolation.

Hall et al.BMJ Pall Care 2011

Lower levels of anxiety and depression at all followup intervals.

Juliao et al.J Pall Medicine 2014

Increased sense of meaning, less regret, and sense of leaving something for future generations.

Vuksanovic et al.J Pain and Symp Mgmt 2017

Find Peace

When you are sick, you don’t feel like yourself, much less have the energy to unearth the meaning in your story. With an active listener, it is possible that you will regain something you thought was lost. Some have found peace by reviewing their life’s narrative. Many variables make up the “dignity equation,” and several words have been thrown around up until this point. It is worthwhile to “unpack” these words and clarify their meaning.

Dignity means, simply put, that I have worth and hold respect.

Peace comes from the realization that there are very few things we have control over, and resting in that.

Legacy is an aspect of myself that remains in the world after I die.

Comfort is the relief of suffering.

Ego-Integrity is the feeling that you did the best you could with what you had, and it had meaning.

Hope is the process by which life continues to feel worth living.
